## Intro
There are various positive and negative aspects to rooting an android. In this post we'll see some of each side, so that you can make your own decision on what to do. Even without rooting your phone there are various things you can do, so only root your phone if you are comfortable with the outcome and feel like you are prepared to.

## Reasons for rooting
There are various good reasons for rooting your android phone. These range from installing a custom ROM to removing bloatware.

### Installing a custom ROM
A custom ROM is a modified android operating system that can be put on your phone. This may give you extra functionality, security, and may remove bugs. Installing a custom ROM may also allow you to upgrade your phone to a later version of android. So if your phone is old and only supports up to, say, android 8 (Oreo) and you want to upgrade it to android 10 (Q) or android 11 (R), installing a custom ROM such as Lineage may allow you to complete this, provided your phone is supported by the manufacturer of the custom ROM.
Custom ROMs may also come without bloatware such as Google Drive, Google Docs, Microsoft Office, etc. (although they will generally include low-size system apps).
