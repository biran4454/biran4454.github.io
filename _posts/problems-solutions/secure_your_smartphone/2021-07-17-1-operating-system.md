### System
**Problem:** Android phones run a version of Linux, and although Linux is generally considered a secure operating systems there are always problems with any system. Problems most often occur in older models of phones, as they are often unsupported by the company, meaning there will be no more updates to their security systems.
Furthermore, they are almost always changed to include the manufacturer's choice of applications and settings. For example a Google phone will come preinstalled and integrated with all Google apps, and there is no option to remove them easily and safely. The settings in a Google phone also rely on their services, and connect into most of the apps. While this can be useful to some, many people are concerned about Google's data collection policies.<br />
**Solution:** There are various solutions to using the pre-installed system. One solution is to install Lineage, a secure operating system that is very similar to standard Android versions. There are various positive aspects to this. It allows your device to run later versions of Android. So if you have an old device that only supports up to Android 8, it may be possible to upgrade it to Android 10 or even 11 by using Lineage. Another positive of Lineage is that the user has control over what apps and services they use. Although it does come pre-installed with system programs, they are all open source and do not interfere with the main system in the way that many apps do.<br />
However Lineage is only accessable if the phone is rooted, which may reduce security if not handled properly.
